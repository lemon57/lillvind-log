After a long winter on the hard, the boat is back on the water.

![Lillvind lowered into the water on launch day]({{ '/assets/images/lanch-day-lillvind-2026.jpg' | relative_url }})

## Winter Work

The maintenance we finished before today:

- **Antifouling** — two coats below the waterline to keep growth off the hull.
- **Saildrive service** — oil change, new seals, diaphragm inspected. Regular service on the saildrive is non-negotiable.
- **Cooling system** — raw-water circuit flushed and a new impeller fitted. Better to sort this in the yard than lose the engine underway.

## Splash Day Prep

Met at the yard in the morning and ran through the checklist:

- **Engine ready for a cold start** — fuel primed, raw-water intake clear, throttle at idle.
- **Seacocks** — every through-hull opened and closed to confirm it moves freely and the hose clamps are tight.
- **Mooring lines** — bow, stern, and two springs, coiled and ready to run.
- **Fenders in place** — hung at the rubbing strake to protect the topsides during the lift.

## Into the Water

The crane lifted him off the cradle, walked him across the yard, and lowered him in. He settled on his lines cleanly. Bilges checked and dry. Engine started on the first turn, with a steady stream of cooling water out of the transom.

## Sea Trial

Ten minutes out on open water. Steering was solid, the engine ran smoothly, no leaks, no alarms. Turned around, came back in, and tied him up.

Season officially open.

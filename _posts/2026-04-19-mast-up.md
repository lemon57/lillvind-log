Early start. We were at the harbour by 8 am — Roman with the mainsail from storage, Ivan with the life jackets, half of Roman's car packed solid.

At the harbour we met Eduard, who had come to help with the mast.

## Getting the Mast Ready

First stop was the mast storage. The mast was in good shape — nothing to worry about there. We couldn't find the proper mast transport cart so we made do with an odd one instead. The mast on our boat isn't heavy, and the three of us moved it easily to the area near the crane.

Then we found someone had moored their boat directly in our spot — the exact place we needed to position Lillvind under the crane. We knocked on the hull a few times. No answer. The boat was empty.

We hadn't dragged ourselves out of bed at dawn to cancel the plan over this. Arsenii, who had also joined us for the day, helped us nudge the boat along the pier just enough to make room. Plan A, not Plan B. Nobody went on a free voyage.

## Setting the Mast

Ivan prepared the mast while the rest of the crew brought Lillvind over to the crane position. Arsenii did a clean job of parallel parking her in.

Then — the classic mistake — we nearly forgot to fit the windex to the masthead before lifting. Caught it in time.

We split roles for the lift: Eduard and Roman held the mast foot steady, Ivan worked the crane controls. Roman lashed the mast to the crane with the confidence of someone who has done this many times, which was enough to put Eduard's mind at rest.

No wind, no rain. The mast went up without drama. We damaged the windex a little, nothing critical, we will fix it a little later without removing the mast.

## Out on the Water

With the mast stepped, we started the engine and decided to take her out. A short round on the sea — just enough to shake things down — then back into the harbour. The team docked her smoothly and got her tied up properly.

## Boom and Rigging

Back at the berth we set the boom, did a bit of rigging work, packed everything up, and headed home to our families.

No tea break again. At this point it feels intentional. Maybe next time we don't need actually bring the thermos.
